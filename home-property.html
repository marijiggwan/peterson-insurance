Homeowners

Standard homeowners insurance for single-family homes. Covers your dwelling, personal property, liability, and additional living expenses if your home becomes uninhabitable. We compare across carriers to find the best fit for your home's age, condition, and location.

Erie, Travelers, Liberty Mutual, The Hartford

Renters

Your landlord's policy covers the building — not your stuff inside it. Renters insurance protects your personal belongings, provides liability coverage, and covers additional living expenses if you're displaced. It's affordable and often required by landlords.

Erie, Progressive, Piedmont Mutual

Flood Insurance

Standard homeowners insurance does not cover flood damage. After Hurricane Helene, this distinction became painfully clear for many WNC homeowners. Flood insurance is a separate policy — and if you're near water, on a slope, or in a low-lying area, it's worth having.

National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP), National General

What Helene Taught Us

After the Storm: Lessons for WNC Homeowners

Hurricane Helene was a wake-up call for Western North Carolina. Many homeowners discovered too late that their standard policy didn't cover flooding, that their roof condition affected their claim, or that their coverage limits hadn't kept up with rebuilding costs.

We helped our clients through every step of the claims process — and we learned a lot about what people wish they'd known before the storm hit.

Your Post-Helene Checklist

  • Review whether you have flood coverage — most homeowners policies don't include it
  • Check your dwelling coverage limit against current rebuilding costs (not market value)
  • Understand your deductible — especially any separate wind/hail deductible
  • Confirm replacement cost vs. actual cash value on your personal property
  • Document your belongings with photos or video — before you need to file a claim
  • Know your agent's number by heart — not just your carrier's 1-800 line
Flood Coverage

Flood Insurance Is Not Optional in WNC

Before Hurricane Helene, a lot of people in Western NC assumed they didn't need flood insurance because they weren't in a "flood zone." The reality is that any property can flood — and standard homeowners insurance never covers it.

Flood insurance is a separate policy, typically through the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) or a private carrier. There's usually a 30-day waiting period before coverage takes effect, so you can't buy it when the storm is already coming.

If you're unsure whether you need flood insurance, come talk to us. We'll look at your property's location, elevation, and proximity to water and give you a straight answer — not a sales pitch.
