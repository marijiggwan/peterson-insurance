Life Insurance

Protect the People Who Depend on You

We offer term, whole, universal, and ERIExpress life insurance through Erie Family Life Insurance Company — from affordable term coverage to lifetime whole life policies.

Term Life Insurance

Term life covers you for a specific period of time. It's typically the most affordable option because you're only paying for coverage during the years you choose. If something happens to you during the term, your beneficiaries receive the death benefit.

Available terms: 5, 10, 15, 20, or 30 years
Fixed premiums for the life of your term
Convertible to whole life down the road
Best for: Young families, homeowners with a mortgage, anyone who needs coverage for a defined period at the lowest cost.

Whole Life Insurance

Whole life covers you for your entire lifetime — not just a set number of years. It also builds cash value over time that you can borrow against if you need to. Premiums are fixed and the death benefit is guaranteed.

Coverage from $10,000 to $10 million
Guaranteed cash value you can borrow against
Six payment duration options available
Accelerated death benefit for terminal illness at no extra charge
Best for: Long-term planning, final expenses, leaving an inheritance, building cash value for retirement, covering children for their lifetime.

Universal Life Insurance

Universal life combines the affordability of term with the cash value of whole life. You get flexible payment options and the ability to adjust your coverage amount over time without signing a new contract. Cash value grows with guaranteed interest.

Flexible premium payments
Adjustable coverage without a new contract
Tax-free policy loans and guaranteed interest rates
Best for: People who want permanent coverage with more flexibility than whole life, or who want to adjust coverage as their needs change.

ERIExpress Life

Need coverage quickly? ERIExpress offers term or whole life insurance through a simplified application process — no medical exam, faster approval. It's designed for people who want straightforward protection without a lengthy application process.

No medical exam required
Simple application, faster approval
Available as term or whole life
Best for: People who want quick, easy coverage or who are concerned about passing a medical exam.
Medicare Supplement

Fill the Gaps Medicare Doesn't Cover

Medicare Supplement plans (Medigap) help pay the deductibles, copayments, and coinsurance that Original Medicare leaves behind. We offer Plans A, G, and N through Erie Family Life Insurance Company.

Plan G — Most Comprehensive

For anyone newly eligible for Medicare, Plan G is the most comprehensive option available. It covers nearly all out-of-pocket costs, letting you choose any doctor who accepts Medicare — including specialists — without referrals.

Covers Part A and B copayments and coinsurance
Part A deductible covered
Part B excess charges covered
Foreign travel emergency coverage (up to 80%)
Choose any doctor or specialist who accepts Medicare
Most popular plan. You pay only the Part B deductible — everything else is covered. Premiums are generally lower than the old Plan F by more than the deductible amount, so you save overall.

Plan N — Budget-Friendly Option

Plan N offers strong coverage at a lower monthly premium than Plan G. You'll pay small copays for some office and ER visits, but the trade-off is a noticeably lower premium. A good option if you're healthy and don't visit doctors frequently.

Lower monthly premium than Plan G
Part A deductible and coinsurance covered
Small copay for office visits (up to $20) and ER visits ($50)
Best for: Medicare enrollees who are relatively healthy and want to keep premiums lower while still having meaningful gap coverage.

Plan A — Basic Coverage

Plan A provides the basic benefits to help with copayments for services covered under Medicare Parts A and B. It's the most affordable Medicare Supplement plan option.

Part A and B copayment coverage
Lowest monthly premium
Best for: Anyone who wants basic supplemental coverage at the lowest cost.

Important: You can apply for a Medicare Supplement plan if you're 65 or older and enrolled in Medicare Parts A and B. The best time to enroll is during your one-time Open Enrollment period — the first six months after you turn 65 and are enrolled in Part B. During this window, coverage is guaranteed regardless of health conditions. We can walk you through the timing and help you choose the right plan.
